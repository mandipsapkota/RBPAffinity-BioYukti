The Science of RNA Regulation

RNA binding proteins (RBPs) are the master regulators of the genetic message, ensuring every strand of RNA is processed, stabilized, and delivered to the right location.

RNA Splicing

Precision editing of RNA transcripts to create functional genetic templates.

RNA Stability

Controlling the lifespan of RNA molecules to regulate protein production levels.

Localization

Directing RNA to specific cellular compartments for localized translation.

The Problem

When TDP-43 Goes Rogue

In diseases like Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS), critical RBPs like TDP-43 lose their normal binding affinity. They mislocalize from the nucleus to the cytoplasm, forming toxic aggregates.

This dysregulation of RNA is a fundamental driver of neuron death.
